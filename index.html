About Shirdi

Shirdi Pilgrimage Guide

Until 1918, Shirdi was a sleepy little village tucked away in the interiors of Maharashtra. That year, an old sage died, leaving behind grieving villagers. This revered sage was their mentor, prophet, and guide: Sai Baba of Shirdi.

Today, Shirdi is accessible by rail up to Nashik and Manmad, by road, and even by air. Sai Baba preached no particular religion he believed in and respected all paths.

Sai Baba's Teachings

11 Sacred Promises
1
Whoever puts his feet on Shirdi soil, his sufferings would come to an end.
2
The wretched and miserable would rise into plenty of joy and happiness, as soon as they climb the steps of my Samadhi.
3
I shall be ever active and vigorous even after leaving this earthly body.
4
My tomb shall bless and speak to the needs of my devotees.
5
I shall be active and vigorous even from the tomb.
6
My mortal remains would speak from the tomb.
7
I am ever living to help and guide all who come to me, who surrender to me and who seek refuge in me.
8
If you look to me, I look to you.
9
If you cast your burden on me, I shall surely bear it.
10
If you seek my advice and help, it shall be given to you at once.
11
There shall be no want in the house of my devotees.
